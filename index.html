Rental Value Analysis: What drives rental success in European cities?

DS4200 Final Project

Presented by: Amelia Rogers, Oluwadamilare Akinsola and Quinn Cahill

Introduction

Airbnb, a platform that connects homeowners with travelers seeking rental properties, is a global peer-to-peer service dominating the short-term rental market. Airbnb connects millions of stakeholders across the marketplace: homeowners who are monetizing spare rooms or properties, travelers seeking accommodations, and local communities navigating the presence of tourism on their economies.

Our project seeks to understand what drives Airbnb pricing by uncovering relationships between property characteristics and local economic attributes. By examining how urban cost of living variables interact with listing features, we seek to identify what makes a rental valuable and how these factors vary across different European markets.

As of 2024, the platform hosted 8.1 million listings worldwide and brought in $11.1 in annual revenue, highlighting Airbnb’s considerable dominance in urban markets.With such a large consumer base, Airbnb has faced criticism for contributing to rising rental costs for local residents. Given the flow of demand it channels into cities, the platform can disrupt local housing dynamics and, if unregulated, contribute to market instability and potential harm to communities. By understanding the drivers of rental success, our analysis provides a data-driven foundation for exploring these dynamics across European cities.

About the Data

The dataset contains 51,707 listings across 10 European cities. It features 36 variables, a majority being continuous numeric variables and a smaller mix of categorical variables. The data includes city-level economic data, property characteristics, location metrics, and host details. This dataset is well-suited for our analysis because it combines property level data with economic context, enabling cross-sectional comparisons across market segments. The data is publicly available on Kaggle, uploaded by Youssef Khaled in February, 2025.

Several limitations in the dataset must be considered, as they directly impact the visualizations and resulting conclusions. The dataset lacks granular time-based detail, capturing only a binary distinction between weekdays and weekends. This limits our ability to analyze seasonality, recent events, and other demand factors that strongly influence rental success. The data also reflects a single time period, meaning we cannot examine historical trends, long-term market competition, or how Airbnb’s success strategy evolves over time.

The dataset does not include detailed amenity information, which plays an important role in pricing strategies, limiting our ability to evaluate how amenities influence rental value. Additionally the economic indicators provided in the dataset are aggregated by city-level and could obscure variance between neighborhoods. For example, a city may have a high overall crime index, but still include very safe, high-value neighborhoods. As a result, neighborhood-level differences and community characteristics can show patterns that city-wide averages hide. Overall, our analysis represents a snapshot in time, and future research including more granular data would help validate our findings about what drives rental success in European cities.

Visualizations

The five visualizations below examine Airbnb pricing from different angles, moving from a city-level overview of price distributions toward more granular comparisons of affordability, location effects, and booking timing.

1. The Distribution of Airbnb Prices Across Cities: How Does City-Level Market Context Shape Airbnb Price Distributions?

Description: This box plot shows the full distribution of nightly Airbnb prices across 10 European cities, with cities sorted by median price and price mapped to x-position as a quantitative variable best encoded on a common scale.

Interaction: Hover over any box to see exact median, quartile, and whisker values. Tooltips were included because box plots make precise reading difficult at a glance.

Takeaway:To start, we wanted to determine whether or not location had a significant impact on the listing of AirBnBs. While the dataset included information about distance from metro stations, town centers, and other specific points, this data didn’t mean the quantitative standards for what we intended to be a vague question. Thus, for this visualization, we created a box-and-whisker plot, compiling multiple types of prince data for several different european cities. From this, we could determine that location does indeed play a significant role in the minimum, maximum, and median prices for AirBnBs (with the exact data made visible through tooltips).

2. Nightly Airbnb Prices vs. Local Monthly Net Salary: Do AirBnB Hosts Price Relative to Local Purchasing Power?

Description: This scatter plot maps each city's average monthly net salary to x-position and average nightly price to y-position, with color encoding the affordability ratio on a red-to-green scale for quick visual pattern recognition across cities.

Interaction: Hover over any city to see exact salary, average price, affordability ratio, and listing count. Tooltips were included because color communicates the general scale and pattern but cannot provide precise values per city.

Takeaway: The consumer base for AirBnBs is typically made up of people not from the area like people who need an area to stay at temporarily. However, this understanding then brings the question: Do the prices of AirBnBs depend on their cities, or do the differences presented in our first visualization correlate to another factor we dont see? To investigate, we created an affordability ratio for each city by dividing the average nightly price by the average daily net salary. This tells us how many days of local wages one night actually costs. After creating this scatterplot, which compares the monthly average net salary of our selected cities to the average nightly price of their AirBnBs (with exact data made visible through tooltips), we can see a vaguely upward trend in which the price of an AirBnB generally stays the same around a certain net salary, only spiking in the case of an outlier like Lisbon or as net salary increases drastically. This finding implies that despite the main consumer base being tourists, AirBnB hosts seemingly prefer to set prices not beyond one’s monthly salary.

3. Average Price by Room Type and Distance from City Center: How Does Distance from the City Center Interact with Room Type to Determine Price?

Description: This faceted area chart maps distance from city center to x-position and average price to y-position, with separate panels per room type so individual price decay curves are not obscured by overlap.

Interaction: Click and drag on any panel to select a distance range and see the average price and percentage of listings in that selection update dynamically below. This gives viewers the flexibility to zoom into specific distance bands and compare price behavior across any custom selection they choose.

Takeaway: Once we’d established a connection between location and price, we moved on to explore the products themselves, or the room types for AirBnBs. Although we assumed there was definitely some connection between the rooms and the price, we wouldn’t know what that connection was until it was made visible. This visualization produces three line/area graphs side by side, comparing their average prices per room type to their distance from their city’s center (so that we also took into consideration some level of location data to align with our previous trend). What we found was a repeated pattern where average price decreases the farther an AirBnB is from a city’s center, with entire homes/apartments having the most drastic slope and shared rooms having the least exaggerated. To better interpret the data being shown, we implemented an additional layer of interaction where parts of the graph could be selected to dynamically view average price and the percent of listings within a selection of the graph.

4. Average Price by Room Type and Distance from Public Transit: Does Proximity to Public Transportation Independently Influence Airbnb Pricing?

Description: This heatmap maps city to y-position and metro distance band paired with room type to x-position, with color intensity encoding average price because the grid structure makes position unavailable for the price variable.

Interaction: Click any cell to filter the linked bar chart below, or shift-click to compare multiple city and room type combinations. This allows viewers to isolate specific segments and directly compare average prices across any combination of city, distance band, and room type.

Takeaway: So far, we’ve shown that price data is affected by the general location of an AirBnB, the distance of the AirBnB from the central area of a city, and the room type of the AirBnB itself. Our next goal was to determine how much transportation played a role in the price listings of AirBnBs. In this visualization, which takes data from the average price total per AirBnB roomtype and applies it to a heatmap based on distance from metro-stations, we can see that prices generally decrease the further away an AIrBnB is from a metro station. In other words, in addition to the location and type of AirBnB, the ease of movement one has around it is also something that may influence its pricing.

5. Guest Satisfaction Scores and Nightly Prices on Weekdays vs. Weekends: Do Weekend Demand Premiums Affect Guest Satisfaction Across European Markets?

Description: This grouped bar chart maps city to x-position and bar height to median price or satisfaction score, with color encoding day type to allow direct weekday to weekend comparison per city.

Interaction: Use the radio buttons to toggle between the price view and the satisfaction score view. This allows viewers to compare whether satisfaction scores differ between weekday and weekend stays and examine whether price changes based on timing correspond to any meaningful change in guest experience.

Takeaway: For our final visualization, we intended to focus on how often customers were satisfied with their AirBnBs given weekend premiums (the period during which AirBnBs are more expensive due to higher demand). This grouped bar chart, which includes a toggle for switching between guest satisfaction scores and weekly prices, shows the general tendency of most AirBnBs to price weekend stays somewhat higher than weekdays. Despite this, our data implies that guests are still content with their visits (or, more accurately, that weekend visits don’t significantly influence their satisfaction), and that these price variations do not have an extreme impact on how a consumer feels about their AirBnB, contrary to initial belief.

Summary & Findings

Our analysis reveals three consistent drivers of Airbnb pricing across European cities.

First, location is the strongest predictor. Both city-level market differences and proximity to city centers drive significant price variation, with homes showing the steepest distance-based price decline

Second, local economic context matters: cities with higher average net salaries command higher nightly rates, suggesting hosts implicitly price relative to local purchasing power.

Third, transportation access (metro proximity) independently influences price across all room types, reinforcing that accessibility is a valued amenity even when controlling for city center distance.

Weekend premiums exist across cities but do not meaningfully reduce guest satisfaction scores, suggesting demand is relatively inelastic to moderate price increases. Future work should incorporate time-series data to capture seasonality, neighborhood-level economic indicators to reduce aggregation bias, and amenity-level detail to isolate the contribution of individual listing features to rental success.
